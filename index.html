About This Page

This is a simple, barebones, material-type design. The colours don't jump too much and they never change. The navigation bar is on the left which will only change when being viewed on a small screen such as a mobile or tablet. When clicking on a navigation link, there is no reload of the page creating an optimized viewing experience. It's easy and quick to design making it ideal for a personal page, resume, or a small, lightweight business.

About Me

I was born in Canada in the year 1980. In my early teen years I quickly developed a taste for computers. However, life has a way of being life and I didn't persue the computer market that I had previously planned. For the past 20+ years I've been working as a cook but as COVID-19 hit the world, I decided to take a little control of myself and used the multiple times off work to learn some skills with the hope of one day working in the ever-growing market.

Growing up, my dad had his own business. Even though I was young and didn't understand the processes of society, it seemed freeing. Since, I've always wanted to work for myself, and with the way the world has been recently, working remotely from home seems like a great idea.

In September of 2020, I enrolled myself into a few courses on the Udemy learning platform. So far I've taken courses in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Node.js (touching on MongoDB), with plans of doing courses in Python, PHP, Java and mobile development.

My Business

My goal is to be a full-stack web developer, which means: I can build what people see as well as the inner workings that people don't see, like handling payments, sending emails from a webpage or even fetching the current weather.

I am just starting out, which means I have no experience in a lot of what this market demands (like working with a dev team). This is a dream of mine and I am willing to put all I can into this, whether it's a volunteer, intern, low-level position, or private contract. I'm quite eager and excited to being on this journey.

For anyone reading this, it should be of note that I am currently working full-time as a cook until I get enough of web design work to support myself.
